🎯 Our Entrepreneurial Journey

Understanding the Youth Employment Crisis

Across Rwanda and many parts of Africa, youth unemployment remains alarmingly high. Despite being the most educated and connected generation, young people often lack access to decent work opportunities, practical experience, or funding to launch businesses.

60% Youth Unemployment Rate
200+ Youth Interviewed
5 Research Methods Used

🔍 Key Research Findings:

📊

Market Gap Analysis

Lack of access to job market information and skills-opportunity mismatch

🎓

Education-Industry Gap

Mismatch between education curriculum and labor market needs

💰

Capital Access

Limited access to funding, credit, and entrepreneurship mentorship

🤔 Key Insights & Learnings

What We Discovered Along the Way

💪

Youth Don't Lack Motivation

They lack tools and access to opportunities

🤝

Peer Learning Works

Especially powerful in areas where mentorship is scarce

🎭

Identity Matters

Career identity is tied closely to dignity and mental health

🗣️

Include Youth Voices

Governments and NGOs must include youth in policy design

🛠️

Skills Over Degrees

Job creation is about skills, networks, and proof-of-work

⚡

Actionable Support

Youth want actionable, not abstract support

💡 Key Revelation

"This journey confirmed that scalable, digital-first solutions—when paired with human support—can shift Africa's youth from job-seekers to job-creators."
